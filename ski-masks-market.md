<p><p>Ski masks are categorized into several types based on their design and coverage. The 3-hole mask provides openings for the eyes and mouth, ideal for warmth and visibility. The 2-hole version covers the face while leaving the eyes exposed, balancing warmth and breathability. Balaclavas offer comprehensive coverage for the head and neck, making them popular for cold-weather activities. Headbands focus on thermal protection for the forehead and ears while allowing for ventilation. Other variations include half masks and helmets that provide unique styling or additional features.</p></p>
