<p><p>The nasogastric intubation market includes various catheter types designed for different clinical uses. The Levin catheter is a single-lumen tube primarily for feeding and medication administration. The Salem sump catheter features dual lumens, allowing for venting and drainage, making it suitable for gastric decompression. The Dobhoff tube is a flexible, weighted feeding tube designed for long-term enteral feeding, enhancing patient comfort and reducing complications. Each type serves specific needs in patient care, influencing market dynamics and demand.</p></p>
