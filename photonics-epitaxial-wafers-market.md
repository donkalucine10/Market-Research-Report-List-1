<p><p>The photonics epitaxial wafers market is primarily segmented into Gallium Arsenide (GaAs) and Indium Phosphide (InP) categories. GaAs wafers are known for their efficiency in optoelectronic applications, such as lasers and solar cells, due to their superior electron mobility and direct bandgap properties, making them ideal for high-frequency devices. In contrast, InP wafers excel in producing high-speed and high-frequency photonic devices, particularly in telecommunications, owing to their lower lattice mismatch with other materials and effective signal transmission capabilities.</p></p>
