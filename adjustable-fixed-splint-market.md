<p><p>The adjustable fixed splint market encompasses three key types: head splints, limb splints, and torso splints. Head splints are designed to stabilize and immobilize head injuries, minimizing movement during healing. Limb splints cater to fractures and strains in arms and legs, providing support and alignment. Torso splints are used for spinal or rib injuries, ensuring proper alignment and reducing pain. Each type is adjustable to accommodate varying patient needs and promote recovery, making them essential in trauma care and rehabilitation.</p></p>
