<p><p>The dental extraction forceps market is categorized into upper and lower extracting forceps, each designed for specific tooth extractions. Upper extracting forceps are typically used for removing maxillary teeth, featuring curved beaks that accommodate the unique shape of these teeth. Lower extracting forceps, on the other hand, are tailored for mandibular teeth, often having straight or angled designs for better access and leverage. The distinction between these types ensures precision and effectiveness in dental extractions, catering to varying clinical needs.</p></p>
