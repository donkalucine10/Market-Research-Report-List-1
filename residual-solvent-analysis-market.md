<p><p>Residual solvent analysis involves detecting and quantifying residual solvents in pharmaceutical and chemical products. The market features two prominent techniques: Gas Chromatography (GC) and Gas Chromatography Mass Spectrometry (GC-MS). GC is commonly used for separating volatile compounds, providing reliable identification of solvents. In contrast, GC-MS combines GC's separation capabilities with mass spectrometry for more precise identification and quantification, enabling comprehensive analysis of complex mixtures. Both methods are crucial for ensuring product safety and regulatory compliance in various industries.</p></p>
