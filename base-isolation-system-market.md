<p><ul><li>Elastomeric Isolator</li><li>Sliding Isolator</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Base isolation systems are crucial for seismic protection in buildings, classified mainly into elastomeric isolators and sliding isolators. Elastomeric isolators are made from layers of rubber and steel, offering flexibility to absorb seismic forces while allowing for building movement. Sliding isolators, on the other hand, utilize low-friction materials to enable horizontal displacement during an earthquake, reducing energy transfer to the structure. Both types enhance safety and minimize damage, catering to diverse architectural needs in seismic-prone regions.</p></p>
