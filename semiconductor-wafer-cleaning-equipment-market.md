<p><p>The semiconductor wafer cleaning equipment market addresses three primary contamination types: metallic, chemical, and particle. Metallic contamination involves unwanted metal ions that can degrade electrical performance, necessitating advanced cleaning processes to ensure purity. Chemical contamination arises from residues of etchants or other materials, requiring specialized solvents and techniques for removal to prevent defects. Particle contamination refers to physical debris (like dust or lint) that can compromise chip integrity, making precision cleaning essential to maintain high yields and device reliability in semiconductor manufacturing.</p></p>
