<p><p>LDMOS transistors, or laterally-diffused metal-oxide-semiconductor transistors, are utilized primarily in two market types: pulse and continuous wave (CW). The pulse market focuses on applications requiring high peak power over short durations, such as radar and communications systems, where rapid signal transmissions are crucial. Conversely, the CW market caters to applications needing consistent power output over extended periods, commonly seen in broadcasting and RF amplification. Each market type leverages distinct performance characteristics of LDMOS transistors to optimize efficiency and effectiveness in their respective applications.</p></p>
